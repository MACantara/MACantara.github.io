Nikola Tesla

Inventor, Scientist, and Innovator

History of Nikola Tesla's Life

  • 1856 - Born in ethnic Serb in the village of Smijan, within the Military Frontier, in the Austrian Empire (Present day Croatia)
  • 1861 - Attended primary school in Smiljan where he studied German, arithmetic, and religion
  • 1862 - Together with his family, they moved to the Gospić, where Tesla's father worked as parish priest.
  • 1870 - Moved to Karlovac to attend high school at the Higher Real Gymnasium
  • 1873 - Graduated from Higher Real Gymnasium
  • 1874 - Evaded conscription into the Austro-Hungarian Army in Smiljan by running away southeast of Lika to Tomingaj, near Gračac
  • 1875 - Enrolled at the Imperial-Royal Technical College in Graz on a Military Frontier scholarship
  • 1878 - Left from Graz
  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest, Hungary to work under Tivadar Puskás at a telegraph company, the Budapest Telephone Exchange
  • 1882 - Worked with the Continential Edison Company in Paris
  • 1884 - Emigrated to the United States and began work at the Machine Works on Manhattan's Lower East Side
  • 1885 - Started on his patent on an arc lighting system
  • 1887 - Formed the Tesla Electric Company with Alfred S. Brown and Charles Fletcher Peck
  • 1887 - Developed an induction moter that ran on alternating current (AC)
  • 1888 - Patented the innovative electric motor
  • 1889 - Created the Tesla Coil
  • 1891 - Became a naturalized citizen of the United States
  • 1891 - Developed wireless lighting
